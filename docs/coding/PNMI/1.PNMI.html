Long-term Plankton and Environmental Monitoring Dataset from the Iroise Marine Natural Park

R
Academic
Data Paper
A 14-year imaging and monitoring dataset revealing plankton community dynamics in France’s first marine protected area
Author

Laetitia Drago

Published

January 7, 2025

This dataset is published on SEANOE and corresponds to the data paper Long-term plankton and environmental monitoring dataset from the marine protected area of the Iroise Marine Natural Park (2010–2023) in the Iroise Sea, North Atlantic (Earth Syst. Sci. Data, 2025).

Dataset Overview

This 14-year monitoring effort (2010–2023) combines phytoplankton and zooplankton observations with environmental measurements from the Iroise Marine Natural Park, France’s first marine protected area. The dataset represents a unique, spatially and temporally resolved record of plankton communities in a region characterized by complex oceanography and significant ecological and economic value.

Sampling zone in the Iroise Marine Natural Park (Figure 1 of the paper).

What’s Included

Phytoplankton data (2010–2022): 785 samples containing 573 unique taxa identified by microscopy at species or genus level. All transect samples were processed by the same experienced taxonomist to ensure consistency across the 13-year period.

Zooplankton data (2010–2023): 650 samples digitized using the ZooScan imaging system, yielding 655,930 individual organism images. These were automatically classified and then validated by expert annotators, resulting in 103 taxonomic and morphological groups with both abundance and biovolume measurements.

Environmental parameters: Temperature and salinity measured at surface and bottom depths across all sampling sites, providing context for interpreting biological patterns.

Sampling design: Two coastal-to-offshore transects (northern transect B with 7 stations, southern transect D with 6 stations) plus three coastal stations (Molène, Sein, Douarnenez). Seasonal sampling increased from three to four times yearly starting in 2017.

Examples of planktonic organisms imaged by the ZooScan for the 20 most abundant taxa (Figure 3 of the paper).

Key Findings

Phytoplankton distribution: Remarkable temporal and spatial variability, with peak abundances (>2 × 10⁶ cells L⁻¹) occurring in 2011 and 2022 for transect B, and 2016 and 2021–2022 for transect D. No consistent coastal-to-offshore gradient was evident when data were temporally averaged, likely because the seasonal Ushant thermal front’s spatial effects are obscured by seasonal averaging.

Zooplankton patterns: Clear coastal-to-offshore gradients with decreasing abundances seaward. Coastal stations (especially B1 and D1) consistently showed highest concentrations, reaching up to 8 × 10³ individuals m⁻³. Copepods and Branchiopods dominated coastal waters, while Mollusca showed inverse gradients with higher offshore proportions. Overall, community structure remained relatively stable across the 13-year period.

Why This Matters

This dataset addresses a significant gap in plankton monitoring for the Iroise region. While other French coastal monitoring programs exist (SOMLIT, REPHY, PELGAS), none provide comparable spatial and temporal coverage of both plankton groups simultaneously within a protected marine area.

The data are particularly valuable because they combine traditional microscopy (phytoplankton) with modern imaging technology (zooplankton), demonstrating how complementary approaches can be integrated and shared. This is especially important given recent findings of declining small pelagic fish body sizes across French waters—understanding their primary food resource (plankton) is essential for fisheries management and ecosystem conservation.

Technical Strengths

  • Taxonomic consistency: Phytoplankton identification by the same taxonomist ensures reliability across the time series
  • High taxonomic resolution: 573 phytoplankton taxa and detailed zooplankton morphological measurements enable trait-based analyses
  • Individual-level data: Complete zooplankton images are publicly accessible via EcoTaxa, allowing future morphometric and machine learning applications
  • FAIR compliance: All code and data follow FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable)

Data Structure

The dataset comprises three tables: one for phytoplankton abundances with environmental context, one for zooplankton abundances and biovolumes with environmental data, and one providing complete taxonomic hierarchies with WoRMS identifiers for unambiguous species identification.

Broader Context

The Iroise Marine Natural Park occupies a unique position at the intersection of the English Channel and Atlantic Ocean, shaped by the seasonal Ushant thermal front that creates distinct coastal and offshore habitats. Beyond its ecological significance, the region supports France’s historic sardine fishery centered in Douarnenez. Long-term plankton monitoring is thus critical for both biodiversity conservation and preserving the economic and cultural heritage that led to the park’s designation.

Perspectives

The dataset opens opportunities for investigating seasonal patterns in plankton community composition, modeling ecosystem responses to environmental gradients, and developing machine learning applications for plankton classification. The individual zooplankton images are particularly valuable for future trait-based studies linking morphological diversity to ecosystem functioning and carbon cycling.
